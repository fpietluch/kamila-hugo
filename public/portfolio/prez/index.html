Introduction to primary aim of the project

Introduction to primary aim of the project

  • Ancient and evolutionarily conserved molecules.
  • AMPs are short (~10-50 amino-acids).
  • Share some common features, such as positive charge, hydrophobicity and amphipathicity.

The primary aim of this project

A new model for plastid evolution with AMPs playing a central role in the endosymbiosis process will be formulated.

  • Endosymiotic gene transfer.
  • Endosymbiont control.
  • Early metabolite exchange
  • Protein trafficking by becoming plastid transit peptides (pTPs).
  • Establishment of plastid translocons along with appropriate proteases for protein import and pTP degradation.

Preliminary studies

Pipeline and review panel:

  • Analyses of transit peptides and comparing with AMPs.
  • Phylogenetic analyses of the major component of the TOC complex.
  • Phylogenetic analyses of proteases responsible for transit peptide processing and degradation.
